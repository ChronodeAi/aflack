# Rule: no-safe-boring-generation

Compliance is necessary but not sufficient. Do not spend generation credits on
asset prompts that are merely clean, generic, or product-adjacent.

Before a prompt reaches spend review, it must prove:

- why the target audience cares in the first frame,
- how the asset relates positively to the current story window,
- what visual tension or contradiction is present,
- what payoff appears by the final frame,
- how the shot can be cut or looped in a Short,
- which marks, logos, likenesses, source footage, and claims are excluded.

For Loadout Lab, a generic controller/headset/storage prompt is not enough. The
shot must be a GTA6 day-one setup failure, audit, reveal, or anti-hype lesson.

For Vice Signal, neon style alone is not enough. The shot must advance the
transmission, bracket, countdown, simulated-world story, or CTA.

# ADR-0006: Virality-first lane selection and persona-optional form

**Status**: Accepted  
**Date**: 2026-07-04  
**Decision owner**: Solo operator

## Context

Across the Claude Code and Codex/Fugu transcripts, the operating doctrine evolved from "make persona content" into "build Memetics as a virality engine." The persona system still matters for brand recognition, but forcing every post through a persona can lower trend fit, slow execution, and make the factory less responsive to high-velocity moments.

The observed creator playbooks also showed that platform payouts are secondary. The real loop is views to audience, audience to trust, trust to brand deals, affiliate offers, lead magnets, software/community funnels, and owned audience assets.

## Decision

Optimize lane and format selection for **views, shares, retention, and monetizable audience growth** before persona continuity.

Personas are optional tools:

- Use **Vice Signal** when GTA6 commentary, AI-world news, hype analysis, or brand continuity improves reach.
- Use **Loadout Lab** when affiliate utility, setup checklists, gear/tools, or buyer-intent content improves monetization.
- Use **persona-free formats** when the trend, meme, explainer, benchmark imitation, or hook pattern is stronger without a character wrapper.

Trend size can beat niche purity, but compliance gates remain binding constraints because account survival is a growth requirement.

## Operating rules

- Treat views/shares/retention as the product signal.
- Prefer fast trend response when the trend has a measurable attention pool.
- Do not preserve persona continuity at the expense of a stronger hook.
- Use personas for recognition, not as mandatory decoration.
- Keep GTA6 pre-release Rockstar footage blocked.
- Keep real-event media at Tier 2 unless the operator signs the Tier 3 risk override.
- Keep AI disclosure and affiliate disclosure visible where required.
- Route audience to owned or portable assets whenever possible: lead magnet, email list, prompt pack, tool referral, community, or product.

## Metrics

Primary creative metrics:

- shares per reach,
- retention and rewatch,
- time from trend to publish,
- hook hold in the first seconds,
- qualified CTA response.

Business metrics:

- cost per publishable creative,
- cost per winner,
- affiliate clicks and conversions,
- lead magnet opt-ins,
- contribution margin,
- MMR/MRR once recurring revenue exists.

## Consequences

### Positive

- Prevents overfitting to lore or persona continuity before the channel has proof.
- Supports FIFA, GTA6, AI-money, B2B flythrough, and affiliate lanes under one doctrine.
- Keeps the creator-commerce framework aligned with business outcomes instead of artifact production.

### Negative / risks

- Brand identity may feel less consistent until winners are identified.
- Requires a stronger performance log to avoid chasing every trend without learning.
- The operator must make explicit lane choices when trend opportunity and risk posture conflict.

### Full-watch-through mechanics (the actual retention engineering)

1. **Open loop at 0s, close it at the last beat.** The hook states a specific promise ("one frame changes everything," "number three is a problem") and the payoff is physically located in the final 3 seconds. The viewer can't get the payoff by skimming.
2. **Visual promise > verbal promise.** In inside-game clips, tease a visual moment ("wait for the chopper") — Higgsfield makes the payoff shot cheap to produce and impossible to predict.
3. **Scene-change cadence every 2–4s.** Impossible camera moves are free for us; each cut re-answers "why keep watching."
4. **Diegetic countdown.** The days-left number appears IN the world (billboard, radio ticker, license plate). Freshness signal + world-building in one asset.
5. **On-screen text that resolves mid-sentence.** Caption text paced so finishing the sentence requires finishing the clip.
6. **Seeded rewatch bait.** Hide one detail per clip and SAY a detail is hidden (IG6). Rewatches + comments are the two strongest ranking signals.
7. **Seamless loop construction.** Last frame ≈ first frame → autoplay replays register as additional watch time on IG/TikTok.
8. **Serialized debt.** End on a forward promise ("Transmission 005: the marina"), not a closed loop. The lordwinanim pattern — retention across clips, not just within.

### The clickbait rule (honest-clickbait doctrine)

Clickbait the **tone**, never the **facts**. The hook may be maximally dramatic, but the specific claim must be repaid in-clip with something real — a verifiable trailer detail, a real spec, a genuine calculation. Unpaid curiosity debt = retention collapse on the back half = algorithm suppression on the next post. The countdown, the record pre-orders, the fps controversy are all REAL drama; we never need to invent any.

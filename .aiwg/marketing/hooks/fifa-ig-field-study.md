## FORMAT LEADERBOARD

1. **Emotional underdog edit-montages**
   - Best examples: @433 Cape Verde pride, Vozinha dream story, Messi/Vozinha respect
   - Top range observed: 19.8M to 35.6M views
   - Why it works: This format turns a match result into a story people can share without needing tactical context.

2. **AI / absurd meme remix**
   - Best example: @433 + Terry Crews “Imagine them recreating it”
   - Top observed: 40M views
   - Why it works: Surreal crossover + recognizable celebrity + football discourse = highest ceiling.

3. **GOAT worship/fan-crowd clips**
   - Best examples: Ronaldo hotel/balcony, Messi POTM, Argentina fans in Miami
   - Top range: 6M to 14.6M views on big accounts
   - Why it works: Messi/Ronaldo still create instant context. The audience already knows why it matters.

4. **Official award/POTM/stat posts**
   - Best examples: Messi POTM, Lamine Yamal POTM, Luis Díaz POTM
   - Top range: 1.5M to 10.5M views
   - Why it works: Clean, shareable, but less meme-native unless attached to Messi or a young star.

5. **Influencer reaction overlays**
   - Best examples: IShowSpeed Ronaldo/Colombia clips
   - Top range: 6.4M to 7.2M views
   - Why it works: The creator’s reaction is the hook, not just the football.

6. **Fan Festival / city / matchday setup clips**
   - Best examples: Miami here we go, Canada vs Morocco, Game on in Kansas City
   - Top range: 700K to 4.3M views
   - Why it works: Useful for official hype, but generally lower viral ceiling than emotional or meme edits.

7. **Talking-head / debate / ranking content**
   - Best examples: team rankings, old long-range goal debates
   - Top range: 1.5M to 3.2M views, but likes can be weak
   - Why it works: Comment bait, but not as visually viral as match/emotion/meme formats.

1. **Cape Verde vs Argentina is the dominant meme/story cluster**
   - Vozinha’s saves against Messi.
   - Cape Verde’s emotional exit.
   - Cape Verde players asking Messi for selfies.
   - Dibu Martinez acknowledging Cape Verde’s second goal as a “golaso.”
   - Comments repeatedly frame Cape Verde as the moral winner.

2. **Vozinha became the week’s breakout character**
   - FIFA: “A name this World Cup won’t forget.”
   - 433: dream of playing Messi, 8 saves, mother flown in, respect from Messi.
   - The meme is not just “great goalkeeper,” it is “40-year-old underdog hero meets Messi.”

3. **Messi content still massively outperforms normal World Cup content**
   - Messi/Cape Verde/Vozinha posts reached 6M, 11.7M, 19.8M, 21.2M views on sampled major accounts.
   - Even official Messi POTM content had comments arguing for Vozinha, which increased debate energy.

4. **Ronaldo content is strong but, in this sample, slightly behind the Messi/Cape Verde cluster**
   - Ronaldo hotel/balcony/fan clips reached 806K, 6.4M, 11.6M, 14.6M views.
   - The best Ronaldo angle is not match footage; it is crowd worship and “Ronaldo effect.”

5. **Brazil/Vini/Haaland/AI absurdity created the single highest outlier**
   - The Terry Crews / “A Thousand Miles” AI remix hit 40M views.
   - This suggests meme remixes can beat real match footage when they attach to a heated national/team discourse.

6. **Lamine Yamal and Endrick are strong secondary star narratives**
   - Lamine Yamal POTM/first World Cup goal: 10.5M on 433.
   - Endrick/Brazil flair: 14.9M on 433.

7. **Fan culture works best when paired with a GOAT or underdog**

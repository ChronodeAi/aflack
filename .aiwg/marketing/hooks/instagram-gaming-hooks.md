## Patterns (observed across the sample)

1. **Question hooks dominate** — "How big do you think…," "Who needs a controller upgrade?," "What do y'all use…" Invite comments; the viewer mentally answers.
2. **Countdowns create instant urgency** — "146 days," "less than 150 days," "November 19, 2026." The date itself is the hook.
3. **Contradiction / news shock** — "Physical copies… but only a digital download," "not expected to run at 60 FPS." A reason to stop.
4. **Speculation is the core GTA6 engine** — file size, Trailer 3 timing, PC release, performance modes, delays. Uncertainty → engagement.
5. **Gear content leans on feature stacking** — "Here's what's packed into…," "Five reasons…," "PC, PlayStation, Xbox…" A product page compressed into one promise.
6. **Challenge hooks in gear/setup** — "Think you've got a sharp eye?" forces rewatching.
7. **Relatable gamer identity** — "Peak male happiness," "I don't need navigation." Sells a feeling, not information.
8. **Authority / pro validation in gear** — pros, esports, "best," "performance edge" framing makes gear feel competitive rather than optional.
